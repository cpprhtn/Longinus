# ⛔ Ethics & authorization — read first

↑ back to [README](../README.md) · doc map: [why](why.md) · [who-its-for](who-its-for.md) · [usage](usage.md)

Longinus is **offense in service of defense.** It is for:

- code/apps **you own or control**,
- targets you are **explicitly authorized** to test (a bug-bounty program scope, a signed
  engagement, written permission), and
- **CTF / deliberately-vulnerable labs**.

It is **not** for testing systems you don't own without permission. [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) enforces an
authorization gate before any active probing, defaults to **read-only / non-destructive** behavior,
and refuses to weaponize for mass-targeting, DoS, malware, or attacks on systems the user doesn't own.
Unauthorized testing is illegal in most jurisdictions (e.g. CFAA in the US, the Computer Misuse Act in
the UK, 정보통신망법 in Korea).

The full rules of engagement, hard-refusal list, scope discipline, and the legal basis with links live
in [references/authorization-and-scope.md](../references/authorization-and-scope.md) (legal references:
[research/process.md](../research/process.md)).
